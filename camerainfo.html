How does the camera work?

In a film camera, light passes through the lens and hits the photosensitive film inside the camera. The film is coated with light-sensitive chemicals that react to the incoming light, capturing an image as a latent (invisible) image.

Process:
- Exposure: When you take a picture, light hits the film, causing a chemical reaction that records the image.
- Developing: The film is submerged in a series of chemical baths to make the image visible.
- Fixing: A fixer chemical stabilizes the image, preventing further exposure to light.
- Printing: If the film is negative (common in traditional film photography), it is used to create a positive print on light-sensitive paper.

In digital camera, it works similarly to a film camera but replaces film with an image sensor (either CMOS or CCD)

Light enters the lens; the lens focuses light onto the image sensor. Image sensor captures the light; the sensor, made up of millions of tiny photosites (pixels), converts light into an electrical signal.
Image processing; the camera's image processor (such as Canon’s DIGIC or Sony’s BIONZ) processes the raw sensor data, adjusting brightness, contrast, and colour.
Image Storage; the processed image is saved as a JPED, RAW, or other format on a memory card/SD card.

Real-Time Display— unlike film cameras, digital cameras allow you to view the image immediately on the
LCD screen or electronic viewfinder.

When was the first camera ever made?

Joseph Nicephore’s heliograph was the first successful attempt at capturing an image using a camera obscura and a light-sensitive material, though the process required extremely long exposure times. His most famous surviving image, View from the Window at Le Gras (1826 or 1827), is the earliest known permanent photograph.

Louis Daguerre built upon Niepce’s work and, in 1839, introduced the daguerreotype process. It significantly reduced exposure times and produced sharper, more detailed images on silver-plated copper sheets. The daguerreotype became the first widely adopted photographic technique, marking the true beginning of modern photography.

What is photography about and what special about it?

Photography is a powerful form of communication that transcends cultural and linguistic barriers, acting as a universal language. In today’s digital era, social media platforms like Instagram and Snapchat have amplified its influence, enabling people to share experiences and emotions instantly.

Photography not only captures moments but also fosters connections, promotes empathy, and highlights social issues, such as environmental protection. As the world becomes increasingly globalized, photography remains a crucial tool for storytelling, reflecting societal trends, and evolving with time.

How photography helps people in their daily life?

Photography plays an important role in people’s daily lives by allowing them to shape and express their identities, document meaningful moments, and connect with others. Self-portraits, or selfies, have become a major part of online culture, helping individuals present themselves in ways that reflect their personalities and aspirations.

Beyond self-expression, photography also enables people to challenge stereotypes and celebrate diversity. By capturing and sharing images that represent different perspectives, individuals can contribute to social awareness and advocate for equality. Moreover, photography influences how we see ourselves and others, shaping perceptions and emotions both consciously and unconsciously.

On a personal level, photography helps people preserve memories, tell stories, and reflect on their experiences. Whether through professional photography or casual snapshots, images serve as a powerful tool for communication, self-discovery, and personal growth. In a rapidly changing world, photography remains an essential medium for defining identity and fostering connections in everyday life.
